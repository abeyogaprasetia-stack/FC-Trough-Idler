Maximize throughput and reduce spillage with trough idler conveyors engineered for safe, reliable bulk material transport in demanding environments. Angled troughing idlers bend the belt into a U-shape that pulls material toward the center, containing small, loose, dusty, or wet loads across long horizontal runs.

High-quality construction: Built with robust structural steel and precision tolerances to handle heavy loads in abrasive applications.

Fully customizable: Choose from a range of sizes, layouts, belt types, and features to match your material handling requirements.

Trough Idler Conveyor FAQ

Trough idler conveyors are pulley-driven and support the belt with angled troughing idlers that bend it into a U-shape. Unlike flat belts, the trough forces material toward the center, containing small, loose, dusty, or wet bulk loads. They are common at mines, quarries, power plants, cement plants, and waste management facilities.

Yes. Trough idler conveyors run at standard incline angles of 25–35 degrees, with custom angles available. Inclined runs use cleated or textured belts to capture material and prevent rollback. See our incline conveyors page for configuration options across angles and belt types.

Yes. The trough profile contains aggregates, coal, grain, and similar bulk materials during the swiveling motion, which makes it well suited to stockpiling. Learn about trough idler radial stacker conveyors for high-volume stacking applications.

Trough idler conveyors can be equipped with a full range of safety devices for worker protection and regulatory compliance, including mushroom-head and pull-cord e-stops, dead man plates, protective guarding, and safety sensors. We design all safety systems to CEMA and OSHA standards and can accommodate facility-specific requirements.

Periodic inspection of idlers, belt tension, belt tracking, pulleys, and drive components keeps these conveyors running. Idlers should be checked for bearing wear and misalignment, since improper tension and tracking are leading causes of premature belt wear. See the trough idler belt conveyor owner's manual for full schedules.

Our average lead time is 10–12 weeks for a custom troughed belt conveyor. Yours may vary depending on configuration and other factors. Contact the Fluent Conveyors engineering team for a timeline tailored to your project.

Our powder coating process can match virtually any color across all structural steel, including side framing, guarding, supports, and hardware. Provide a RAL number, a paint name and brand, or a physical sample. Let your sales rep know during design. See our paint process for details.

Trough idler conveyors excel at moving granular and abrasive bulk materials over distance. Their U-shaped profile contains loose loads that flat belts would spill, optimizing them for aggregates and other dense, free-flowing materials where centering and dust control matter.

Yes. Trough idlers are a standard choice for long-distance conveyance of rock, ore, and gravel. See how we configure conveyors for mining to handle high tonnage, abrasive loads, and the alignment demands of extended runs.

Trough idler conveyors are built for sustained, high-throughput bulk handling, where belt width, troughing angle, and drive sizing are matched to your tonnage targets. Learn more about selecting conveyors for bulk materials and how containment reduces spillage and fugitive dust.
